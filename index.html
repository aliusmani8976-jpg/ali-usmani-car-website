üöÄ The Beginning (1937)

Founded by Kiichiro Toyoda, Toyota Motor Corporation began as a spinoff from his father's company Toyota Industries to create automobiles. The company was established on August 28, 1937, and has since grown into a global automotive powerhouse.

üåü The Toyota Production System

Revolutionizing manufacturing worldwide, TPS (Toyota Production System) introduced concepts like Just-In-Time production and Kaizen (continuous improvement). This philosophy eliminated waste and maximized efficiency, becoming the gold standard in manufacturing.

Global Expansion

From the first Corolla in 1966 to becoming the world's largest automaker, Toyota has sold over 10 million vehicles annually. The company operates in over 170 countries with more than 370,000 employees worldwide.

üîã Hybrid Revolution

In 1997, Toyota launched the Prius, the world's first mass-produced hybrid vehicle. Today, Toyota has sold over 20 million hybrid vehicles globally, leading the charge toward sustainable mobility.
